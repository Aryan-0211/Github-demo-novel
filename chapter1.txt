In Chapter 1 of *The Great Gatsby*, Nick Carraway, the narrator, introduces his background and moves to West Egg to start a career in finance. He lives next door to the enigmatic Jay Gatsby, who is known for his grand mansion and lavish parties. Nick also visits his cousin Daisy and her husband, Tom, in East Egg. During the visit, Nick senses the tension between them, especially as Tom's infidelity with Myrtle Wilson becomes evident. As the evening progresses, Nick observes Gatsby standing outside, staring longingly at the distant green light, symbolic of his unattainable dreams, particularly his desire for Daisy. This chapter sets the stage for the exploration of themes such as love, social class, and the pursuit of the American Dream.
